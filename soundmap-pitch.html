01 — Creator Sourcing

Where to find the right creators

Focus on 3–4 channels that already map onto Soundmap's core behavior.

Channel 01
Music Discovery Creators on TikTok
Already posting "songs you need to hear" content. Right audience, right behavior — easy to brief and highly relevant.
Channel 02
College Creators
Soundmap is location-based. College communities are tight-knit and campus-centric — perfect fit for local music discovery.
Channel 03
Street Interview Format Creators
"What are you listening to?" creators. High comment rates, naturally invite participation. Format maps directly onto Soundmap's social layer.
Channel 04
Music Meme & "Put Me On" Pages
Communities already asking for recommendations and debating music taste. Soundmap is the destination — CTA writes itself.
02 — Creator Criteria

What makes a good Soundmap creator

Follower count is secondary. Content style is everything.

Comfortable talking about music on camera Can speak naturally about songs and vibes without a script feeling forced.
High comment-to-view ratio Comments signal debate, tagging, participation — exactly what Soundmap needs.
Already makes interactive or opinion-based content Polls, rankings, "would you rather" formats. Their audience already knows how to participate.
Posts consistently Reliability matters more than viral potential. You need steady output, not one big post.
Gen Z / music-fan audience — 10k to 200k followers Sweet spot for UGC authenticity. Big enough to matter, small enough to feel real.
03 — Team Management

Running a UGC creator system

Simple structure. Give creators themes, not scripts — so content stays native to their voice.

Tool 01
Slack / Discord Creator Hub
Central space for briefs, feedback, and inspiration clips. Keeps everyone aligned without email chaos.
Tool 02
Weekly Prompt List
3–5 themes each week. Creators pick the one that fits their voice. A theme feels native. A script feels like an ad.
Tool 03
Shared Idea Bank
Creators can drop ideas and claim prompts. Builds ownership and keeps content feeling genuine.
Tool 04
Content Leaderboard
Track views, comments, and installs per creator. Top performers get more budget and early feature access.
Example weekly prompts:  "Songs you'd fight someone over"  ·  "Songs only your hometown knows"  ·  "Songs you gatekeep"
04 — Content Formats

Video concepts — Part 1

Every concept is a conversation starter. They drive comments, tags, and debates — that's how music content spreads.

Concept 01
"What are you listening to?" — but drop it on Soundmap
Street interview format. Instead of just naming the song, pull up the app. Shows the product live, feels organic.
Concept 02
Guess the song from the first second
High-comment format. People will fight in the comments. Works perfectly as a Soundmap challenge.
Concept 03
Songs that would start a fight in the group chat
Debate-bait. Drives tags and shares. "Drop yours on Soundmap" as the CTA.
04 — Content Formats

Video concepts — Part 2

Location-native and identity-driven formats that make Soundmap's core value prop visible.

Concept 04
Rate my Soundmap collection
UGC loop — creator shows their map, asks followers to rate. Incentivizes people to build their own.
Concept 05
Songs people pretend to like
Hot take format. High shareability. Audience immediately thinks of someone to tag.
Concept 06
Songs that hit different depending on where you are
Directly ties to Soundmap's location angle. "The song only sounds right when you're in [city]."
Concept 07
Put me on to a song only your city knows
Community sourced. Pulls in local pride and discovery — Soundmap's core value prop in one prompt.
05 — TikTok Trends

Trends applicable to Soundmap

Music apps thrive on opinion content. These formats are already working — Soundmap fits naturally inside them.

"Put me onto…" Trend Creators ask followers to recommend songs in comments. Massive discovery format. Soundmap is literally the app for this — the CTA writes itself.
POV Music Taste Videos "POV: you have impeccable music taste." Highly shareable identity content. Soundmap lets people show their taste visually through their collection.
Hot Take Format "Your favorite artist only has 3 good songs." People can't scroll past a wrong opinion. Music apps thrive here.
Also trending: blind song ranking · "songs that never miss" · location-based music content ("sounds that define a city") — all map directly onto Soundmap's features.
06 — Platform Strategy

TikTok vs Instagram

TikTok is where you find new audiences. Instagram is where you keep them.

TikTok — Discovery Engine

  • Trends and sound-based hooks
  • Street interviews and hot takes
  • Opinion content that sparks debate
  • Unfiltered, creator-native delivery
  • CTA: "Drop your song on Soundmap"

Instagram — Community Engine

  • Best TikToks reposted as Reels
  • Carousel music debates (swipe format)
  • Stories for polls and direct CTAs
  • Creator reposts and DM-able content
  • Slightly more produced content is fine here
One-line POV: Run discovery on TikTok. Run retention and community on Instagram. Don't treat them the same.
07 — Competitive Inspiration

Apps doing organic content well

Shows you understand platform-native growth — not just how to run ads.

Duolingo — Personality-Driven Content Mascot has a voice, takes hot takes, responds to pop culture. Lesson: give your content a personality, not just a feature. People follow characters, not apps.
BeReal — Community Participation Made the community the content. No influencers needed — just your friends being real. Lesson: design content so users naturally become the creators.
Spotify — Culture & Music Commentary Wrapped became a cultural moment. Lesson: make the data personal and shareable. Soundmap can do this with location data and music history.
