Soundmap — Deliverable Prototype

Format: "Moments Reveal"
Hook: viral clip → zoom out → app collectible reveal
Core

Start with a widely-recognized viral clip. Then reveal it’s a Moment inside Soundmap — someone collected or traded it. Reaction is collectible-native, not an ad.

Why it works
  • Hook relies on cultural recognition, not a pitch.
  • Native app reveal feels organic.
  • Format is infinitely reusable across clips.

Series
  • Rare Moment Pull — reacting to unlocking a rare Moment via trade.
  • I Traded for This — show what you gave up and debate value.
  • This Moment Just Dropped — race to collect a clip that just went viral.

Sourcing Creators & Criteria
Practical approach
Acquisition
  • Start with micro + mid-tier creators (10k–200k). High engagement beats follower count.
  • Search signals: high watch-through, frequent reactions, niche cultural verticals (sound, clips, nostalgia).
  • Criteria: fast turnarounds, consistent style, willingness to reuse template scripts, clear rights/UGC terms.
  • Outreach: brief creative brief + one paid test piece, then scale top performers into content pods.
Managing a Team of UGC Creators
Structure + ops
Ops
  • Organize creators into pods (by vertical). Assign briefs and KPI windows (views, collect rate, trade mentions).
  • Provide templates: 3–5 short scripts + B-roll examples; clear deliverable checklist.
  • Weekly reviews: highlight top clips for reuse and rapid iteration.
  • Comp structure: pay per accepted post + performance bonus for high collect/engagement.
TikTok vs IG & Other Apps
Channel strategy
Channels
TikTok
  • Short, punchy hooks; vertical native reactions; trends + sound usage are critical.
  • Favor unfiltered, authentic reactions and quick call-to-action: "Collect on Soundmap."
Instagram
  • Use Reels for short-form; carousels for deeper storytelling (e.g., trade thread), and Stories for CTAs and links.
  • IG tolerates slightly more produced content; use captions to add context about the trade/story.
Other apps (X, Facebook, YouTube Shorts)
  • Repurpose highest-performing clips; adapt captions and CTAs per platform norms.
